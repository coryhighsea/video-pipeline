## HOOK

- Germany had a deadline — March 6th, 2026
- BSI registration — ~30,000 companies expected
- ~11,000 registered. That's 1 in 3
- Is this a compliance crisis, or a more complicated story?
- Honest answer: both — let's look at it properly

---

## WHAT REGISTRATION ACTUALLY IS

- Important to frame this correctly before talking about the numbers
- Registration is NOT an audit, NOT a certification, NOT proof of compliance
- It's a form — company name, sector, entity type, contact person
- Takes ~30 minutes
- So: 11,000 companies completed a 30-minute form. 19,000 didn't.
- If they haven't done the easiest step — what does that say about the deeper work?

---

## WHY SO FEW? (4 reasons — take your time on each)

**1. Awareness is genuinely low**
- BSIG came into force December 2025 — not long ago
- BSI portal only opened January 2026 — 2 month window
- Big enterprise with legal team: tight but doable
- Mid-sized food company, waste management firm: 2 months is almost nothing
- NIS2 hits sectors that have never been cybersecurity-regulated before — they're not reading BSI bulletins

**2. Scope uncertainty**
- "Are we even affected?" — most common question we hear
- The test sounds simple: 50+ employees or €10M revenue, in 1 of 18 sectors
- In practice: grey areas everywhere — subsidiaries, mixed businesses, "do we count as digital infrastructure?"
- BSI guidance exists but someone has to read it, interpret it, decide
- That friction causes delay

**3. Deliberate wait-and-see**
- Some companies know they're in scope — and are choosing to wait
- No company has been fined yet under the BSIG
- On a pure short-term risk calculation, it's not irrational
- But it is a bet — and worth being clear about what you're betting on

**4. The deeper work feels overwhelming**
- Registration is step one — but companies look ahead and see: 132 requirements, 12 categories, personal liability, €10M fines
- So they freeze on step one because step one feels like the start of something they're not ready for
- Psychological barrier, not a practical one — but very real

---

## WHAT THE BSI DOES NOW

- Short answer: not much immediately — and that's honest, not reassuring
- They're not issuing 19,000 fine notices next week — enforcement infrastructure takes time
- But three things to understand:

- **The list** — BSI now has a registry of who showed up. Conspicuous absences in regulated sectors = future scrutiny
- **The fines** — Essential entities: up to €10M or 2% global turnover. Important entities: €7M or 1.4%. Management personally liable — not the company, the individual
- **The powers** — BSI can request documentation, conduct audits, issue binding instructions. These exist now. Question is pace, not direction.

- Bottom line: early enforcement window. It will close.

---

## WHAT NON-REGISTERED COMPANIES SHOULD DO

- Don't assume the deadline passing means you're off the hook — it doesn't
- Late registration is better than no registration — portal is still open, register now
- Don't let the big picture paralyze you on the easy steps — registration is a form, do it
- Do a gap analysis — 12 categories aren't equal, some you'll already have in practice, just undocumented
- Key mindset: NIS2 doesn't require perfect security — it requires *documented* security
  - A company with great security and nothing written down = non-compliant
  - A company with documented imperfect processes = on the right track
  - The documentation IS the work
- If you don't know where to start — that's normal, most companies in scope are in the same position

---

## WHAT THIS TELLS US

- Large regulatory rollouts into new sectors are hard — this is a cultural shift, not just a compliance task
- The gap between a law passing and enforcement ramping up always creates a window where non-compliance feels safe — that window is temporary
- Germany isn't uniquely bad — other EU states had similar patterns
- But Germany has a huge Mittelstand in exactly the sectors NIS2 targets — the exposure is real
- 11,000 registered are ahead. The 19,000 who didn't — the longer they wait, the harder the catch-up
