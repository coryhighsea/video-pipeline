## WHAT IS NIS2? (0:20–1:10)

NIS2 is a European cybersecurity directive. It came into force across the EU in late 2024 and was implemented in Germany in December 2025 through national law called the BSIG.

Here's the core of what it does:

It requires companies in 18 specific sectors to document how they manage cybersecurity risks — and to register with the national cybersecurity authority. In Germany that's the BSI.

That's the short version.

Why does this law exist? Because too many companies in critical sectors had no documented incident response plan, no clear ownership of cybersecurity decisions, and no visibility into the security practices of the vendors they depended on.

A cyberattack on one company in a critical sector can cascade into a sector-wide event. NIS2 is the EU's answer to that systemic risk.

## ARE YOU AFFECTED? (1:10–2:10)

Two questions determine whether your company is in scope.

Question one — size: Does your company have 50 or more employees, or at least 10 million euros in annual revenue?

Question two — sector: Does your company operate in one of these 18 sectors?

Energy. Transport. Banking. Financial market infrastructures. Health. Water. Wastewater. Digital infrastructure. ICT service management. Public administration. Space. Postal and courier services. Waste management. Chemicals. Food. Manufacturing — certain types. Digital providers. And research.

If both answers are yes — your company is almost certainly in scope and has compliance obligations.

## WHAT DOES IT ACTUALLY REQUIRE? (2:10–3:30)

Three things.

**First: Registration.**
You need to register your company with the BSI. They opened a dedicated portal for this in January 2026. It's a form — your company name, sector, entity classification, and a contact person. It's not an audit. Registration is where you start.

**Second: Documentation.**
NIS2 defines 132 requirements across 12 categories. Governance, risk management, incident response, business continuity, supply chain security, access controls — and more.

Here's something important: NIS2 does not require you to have perfect cybersecurity.

It requires you to have documented, evidenced cybersecurity practices.

A company with strong but undocumented security is non-compliant. A company that has documented its processes — even imperfect ones — is on the right path.

The documentation is the actual work.

**Third: Incident reporting.**
If a significant cybersecurity incident affects your operations, you're required to report it. Early warning to the BSI within 24 hours. Full notification within 72 hours. Final report within 30 days.

The key is having a documented process in place before an incident happens — who makes decisions, who contacts the BSI, what information gets reported.

## WHAT SHOULD YOU DO? (3:30–4:20)

If you're in scope and haven't started — you're not alone, and it's not too late.

The most useful thing you can do right now is confirm whether you're actually in scope. Then register with the BSI. Then work through the 12 categories one at a time, starting with governance.

You don't need to become a cybersecurity expert. You need someone in your organisation who is organised, thorough, and has the authority to coordinate across teams.

This is a project management challenge. Not a technical one.

And it's the same kind of documented, governed discipline you already apply to financial controls or health and safety. NIS2 extends that to cybersecurity.
